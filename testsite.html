Why You Should Never Share Your Browser Cookies

Access to Sensitive Information: Cookies often store sensitive data like login credentials, session tokens, and personal preferences. Sharing these cookies can give others access to your accounts on various websites, including email, social media, and banking platforms. Tracking and Profiling: Cookies often track your browsing history to create a profile of your online activities. Sharing your cookies could expose your browsing habits, interests, and personal data to others, compromising your privacy. Session Hijacking: Cookies are essential for maintaining active sessions on websites. If someone else accesses your session cookies, they can hijack your session, allowing them to perform actions as if they were you.
